First Reflection

The idea from Susan Sontage’s Plato’s Cave is a new idea to me viewing the image taking. She talked about the power of photography and image making, which is using it for recording incriminates, and looking at the world through the photo, like traveling. Personally, to capture images and create graphics is because I want to record the moment and scenery that I like, as it stores in the camera or in the photo album, and it will become part of my memory storage. I also agree that photography promotes nostalgia. If one day I forgot what I saw before, the image will become the evidence or the key to the locker of my memories storage, and then it will remind me about the time and the scene of the image again. She also brings the idea about the emotion connected to photography as she mentioned the Vietnam War. I found it painful to see someone suffering, it reminded me of the picture about the Vietnam War in the last lecture. A few soldiers behind those kids, and those kids looked like they were lost and in pain. Maybe the photographer expresses the idea in a brutal way, to record someone’s pain, make the public hate the war and against the war. The emotion connected to the picture is pretty strong. Photography is also the history itself. Photography also plays a role or meaning in different cultures. In China, black and white photos symbolize the past and even death. Only dead people have black and white portraits. But it has a different meaning in the Western World. As photography always reflects reality, photography exposes more of the dark side that we can't imagine, since photos are often more shocking than hearsay.
